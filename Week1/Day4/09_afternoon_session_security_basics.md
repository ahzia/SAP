### What is Information Security?

**Definition:** Information security involves protecting information and information systems from unauthorized access, use, disclosure, disruption, modification, or destruction.

**In Simple Terms:** Keeping information safe from people who shouldn't have it.

**Key Goals:**
- **Confidentiality** - Only authorized people can see it
- **Integrity** - Information is accurate and not changed
- **Availability** - Information is available when needed

**Think of it as:** Locking your house to protect your belongings.

---

### Why Security Matters

**Personal Level:**
- Protect your personal information
- Keep your accounts safe
- Prevent identity theft

**Business Level:**
- Protect customer data
- Protect company secrets
- Comply with laws
- Maintain trust

### Three Key Security Concepts

#### 1. **Authentication**
**What it is:** Proving who you are
**How it works:**
- Username and password
- Fingerprint
- Face recognition
- Security questions

**Example:** Logging into your email with password

**Think of it as:** Showing your ID to prove who you are

---

#### 2. **Authorization**
**What it is:** What you're allowed to do
**How it works:**
- Different users have different permissions
- Some can view, some can edit
- Some can delete, some cannot

**Example:** Employee can view reports, but only manager can approve expenses

**Think of it as:** Having a key to certain rooms, but not all rooms

---

#### 3. **Encryption**
**What it is:** Converting information into code
**How it works:**
- Information is scrambled
- Only people with key can read it
- Protects data in transit and at rest

**Example:** Credit card number is encrypted when you shop online

**Think of it as:** Writing a secret message in code
